FAQs

Questions people ask about mediation

How is mediation different from going to court?

Mediation is usually faster, more affordable and confidential. Both parties shape the outcome voluntarily, which helps preserve the relationship. Litigation is decided by a judge, takes longer and tends to damage relationships.

Is a mediated agreement legally binding?

A signed mediation agreement is a binding contract. It can be made enforceable through the appropriate legal channels in your jurisdiction.

How long does it take?

Most matters resolve within 1–4 weeks — far faster than litigation.

What if the other party won't take part?

We can reach out and explain the benefits. Mediation is voluntary, but most parties also want a fast, low-cost resolution.

Is everything confidential?

Yes. Mediation is private — unlike a public court hearing your information stays protected.
