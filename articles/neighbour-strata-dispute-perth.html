Neighbour & Strata Disputes in Perth: How to Resolve Them Without Court

Community · ~4 min read

A fence line, a barking dog, an overhanging tree, visitor parking, a strata by-law nobody agrees on. Neighbour disputes start small — but unlike a business dispute, you still have to live next to this person tomorrow. That's exactly why the "lawyer up and win" approach so often makes life worse, not better.

The most common disputes we see

  • Dividing fences — who pays, what standard, where exactly the boundary is;
  • Noise — music, dogs, air-conditioners, renovations at the wrong hours;
  • Trees — overhanging branches, roots, leaves, blocked views and light;
  • Parking and access — shared driveways, visitor bays, blocked crossovers;
  • Strata living — by-law disputes, pets, common property use, levies and committee conflict.

Why racing to a tribunal usually backfires

Formal proceedings are slow, public and adversarial. Even when you "win", you've usually bought yourself a hostile neighbour for years — plus months of stress and cost along the way. And enforcement of any order still depends on the other person's cooperation day to day. A resolution the neighbour actually agreed to beats one imposed on them.

💡 Courts and tribunals are the last resort, not the first move. Most neighbour disputes are communication failures wearing a legal costume.

How mediation handles it

  1. Free consultation — tell us what's happening; we assess whether mediation fits.
  2. Private sessions — each side speaks with the mediator for an hour, separately and in confidence. This is where the real concerns surface.
  3. Joint mediation — a structured conversation (minimum two hours) where both sides are actually heard — often for the first time.
  4. Common ground — a practical arrangement both households can live with. Nothing is signed unless both parties want it in writing.

Fees are hourly at an industry-standard rate and split 50/50 — neither side "hires" the mediator, which is precisely why the process stays neutral. If either of you prefers not to meet face to face, online and shuttle formats work too.

What about strata committees?

Strata disputes are group disputes — multiple owners, a committee, sometimes a managing agent. Mediation scales to that: we can facilitate between an owner and the committee, or within a committee that's stopped functioning. It's confidential, so disagreements don't poison the whole scheme's AGMs for years.
