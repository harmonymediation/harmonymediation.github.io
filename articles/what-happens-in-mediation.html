What Happens in a Mediation Session?

Process · ~4 min read

Knowing what to expect removes most of the anxiety. Here's exactly how a mediation with us runs, from first call to resolution.

Step 1 — Free consultation

You tell us what's happening. We assess honestly whether mediation fits — and if it doesn't, we say so.

Step 2 — A private session for each party

Before any joint meeting, each party has a one-hour private session with the mediator. This is confidential: your concerns, your constraints, your real bottom line. The other side gets the same. This is where most of the groundwork for a resolution actually happens.

Step 3 — Joint mediation

Both parties together with the mediator — at least two hours, extendable to four for complex matters. The mediator keeps the conversation structured and forward-looking: issues get named, options get built, gaps get narrowed. It's firm on the problem, respectful to the people.

Step 4 — Common ground, at your pace

If both parties agree, the process repeats until the matter resolves or someone steps away. And here's something that surprises people: nothing is signed unless both parties want it. A written agreement is drawn up only by mutual choice.

Why? Because without the pressure of "you must sign today", people say what they can genuinely accept — and outcomes built on genuine acceptance are the ones that last.

How to prepare

  • Get legal advice beforehand so you know your position and alternatives
  • Bring the key documents; leave the grievance file at home
  • Know your must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
  • Come ready to listen — the other side's constraints often reveal the deal
