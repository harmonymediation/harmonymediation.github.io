Is a Mediated Agreement Legally Binding?

Essentials · ~4 min read

"What if we mediate, they sign, and then they just don't follow through?" It's the most common worry people have — and a fair one. The good news: a mediated agreement is binding, and there are clear ways to make it enforceable.

1. The agreement is a binding contract

When both parties voluntarily reach and sign a mediation agreement, it functions as a contract. It's binding on both sides, and a party who fails to honour it can be held to it.

2. Making it enforceable

To strengthen it further, a settlement reached in mediation can be formalised so that it's directly enforceable — for example, by having it recorded as consent orders or a deed, depending on your jurisdiction and the nature of the dispute. That means if the other side breaches it, you can move to enforcement rather than starting a fresh dispute from scratch.

💡 In short: a mediated agreement, properly drafted and formalised, gives you something you can actually rely on — far quicker than litigating the original dispute.

3. So how worried should you be about a backout?

  • With a signed agreement: you can act on the contract to require performance.
  • With the agreement properly formalised/enforceable: a breach can move straight to enforcement.

The lesson: reaching agreement is step one — locking it in is just as important. An experienced mediator designs that enforceability into the agreement during the session, not after.
